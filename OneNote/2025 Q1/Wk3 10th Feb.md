**WILL KING QUESTIONS!!!**
 
The first experiment that we are intending to do is a reproduction of an experiment on tomatoes which measured airborne acoustic signals due to bubble-formation in the xylem. My thinking was that if there are mechanical vibrations transmitted through the stem, then (in e.g. herbaceous plants at least) there might potentially be a measurable Calcium response in the surrounding tissue.
 
I can see that there might be issues:
 
1) stems might be too thick to allow the exciting radiation to penetrate and escape (maybe this is why I can only see videos of leaves!);
 
2) the acoustic events happen over the course of \<1ms. If the events were to generate a single pulse then from their evidence it could take place over as little as 0.025ms! And it looked like the GCaMP response is going to be at least 1ms, but then again, will that matter if there is a spatial biochemical wave that moves on a slower scale, like in the case of herbivory;
 
3) that if one needed to use a transgenic plant, and it had to be Arabidopsis, there isn't that much 'stem' available to produce AEs to listen to.
