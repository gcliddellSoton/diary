**TITLE: "Phytoacoustics: An Introduction"**  
   
**ABSTRACT:** Plants make noises, from the creak of trees in the wind, to the gurgling rise of sap in the spring. For a long time we have also known that the appearance of embolisms (air-pockets) in the wood can produce acoustic emissions from audible frequencies into the ultrasound range too, and there have been attempts to use these emissions to investigate the physics-defying process of water transport in plants. Only in the 21st Century however, has the main-stream scientific community acknowledged that some plants emit, and might even perceive, sounds through air and soil.  
As we advance through the information age, the prospects of fully characterising these phenomena, and using them for practical purposes have started to become very close to reality. Using 'synthetic' plant sounds produced by specialised ultrasonic transducers, we are exploring the possible methods of detecting these acoustic events in long signals.
