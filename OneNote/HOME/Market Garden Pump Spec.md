- Flow-rate:
 
- If assuming a 2L/hr emitter (fairly standard), then we want around Q = (60*2*34)*2 = 8160Lph = 136Lpm = 2.27L/s if irrigating the beds area all at once. If the beds are split into 2 zones =\> 68Lpm
 
- For the polytunnel overhead, assuming 90Lph spray nozzle, 1 nozzle/m, 2 x 20m lines, Q = 60Lpm (or more!) overall, therefore Q = 30Lpm in a single line. Assume only 2 lines, this keeps it lower than the bed flowrate. Q = 60Lpm
 
- Pressure-wise:  
- The drip irrigation must be low pressure, e.g. 1-2 bar (maybe no pump necessary?)  
- For the overhead irrigation 3 bar probably better. 0.2bar loss in height, given Q = 30Lpm, P_loss \< 1.85bar in the 20m run and need at least 1-2 bar left for the spray nozzles
   

Our ideal pump needs a characteristic curve that is close to 60Lpm @ 2-3bar and 175Lpm @ 1 bar. Add pressure regulators on all Zones on the outlets of the solenoid valves. Isolation valve before pump and possibly bypass valve too.
